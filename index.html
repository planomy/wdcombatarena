Structure Guide

Sentence Starters

Literary Device Self-Check

Metaphor

Saying one thing is another.

"The hallway was a wild zoo."

Imagery

Painting a vivid word-picture.

"Casting long shadows, the sun sank behind the peaks."

Simile

Comparing using 'like' or 'as'.

"Liam stood as still as a stone statue."

Using Senses

Describing sights, sounds, or smells.

"The bitter, icy wind stung his lips."

Personification

Human traits to objects.

"Screaming through glass, the gale demanded entry."

Onomatopoeia

Words that sound like their meaning.

"Hissing violently, the radiator clanked."

Alliteration

Repeating starting sounds.

"The sinister serpent prepared to strike."

Synecdoche

Part represents the whole.

"A fleet of white sails approached the city."

Asyndeton

Skipping conjunctions to speed flow.

"Sweating, panting, bleeding, he dragged himself from the wreckage."

Juxtaposition

Contrasting opposites.

"relentless blizzard outside; delicate fire inside."

Bad Start
Bad Starts

Starting with pronouns (He, She, It, They), Character Names, or The... too often makes your writing feel basic and repetitive.

Try "When"

"Seconds later, Liam..."

"Before anyone could react,..."

"The moment the lights died,..."

Try "Why"

"Fearing for his life,..."

"Desperate to escape,..."

"Certain he was being watched,..."

Try "Although"

"Although his hands shook,..."

"Although the room looked safe,..."

"Although she wanted to run,..."

Try "With"

"With a tight grip on the handle,..."

"With breath trapped in his throat,..."

"With the wind biting his cheeks,..."

Fast Fix List

"Without warning,..." • "In the distance,..." • "Beneath the floorboards,..." • "Against the silence,..." • "Through the crack,..." • "Behind him,..." • "Above the doorway,..." • "Under the streetlight,..."

Name
Character Name

Overusing names makes your writing sound flat. Swap some names for sharp descriptive labels.

Instead of "Liam"

"the wide-eyed boy"

"the nervous runner"

"the silent kid at the back"

Instead of "She"

"the girl with trembling hands"

"the stubborn newcomer"

"the one who never looked away"

Crowd / extras

"a sleepy cashier"

"two arguing tourists"

"a bored security guard"

Quick pattern

"the + adjective + noun"

"the + noun + with + detail"

"the one who + verb"

Example: "Liam" → "the breathless sprinter" → "the kid in the torn hoodie" → "the one who kept checking the shadows"

Basic Word
Basic Vocabulary

Common words don’t push marks up. Pick a stronger verb that matches the mood.

Said ➔ muttered, snapped, pleaded, whispered
Went ➔ strode, sprinted, crept, stumbled
Looked ➔ glanced, stared, scanned, peered
Got ➔ grabbed, snatched, collected, earned
Made ➔ crafted, formed, built, produced
Ran ➔ bolted, charged, dashed, fled
Walked ➔ paced, drifted, marched, shuffled
Big ➔ massive, towering, bulky, enormous
Bad ➔ grim, rotten, brutal, suspicious
Nice ➔ kind, generous, patient, thoughtful
Rule of Thumb

If your verb could fit in every story, it’s probably too basic.

Passive
Passive Voice

Too many was / were sentences sound weak. Make the character do the action.

"The door was opened by Mia."

"Mia yanked the door open."

"The bag was dropped."

"His bag hit the tiles with a slap."

"The room was filled with smoke."

"Smoke crawled across the ceiling."

"He was hit by the ball."

"The ball smashed into his ribs."

Spot it fast

Watch for: was, were, is, are, been, being + (usually) a past-tense verb.

Filter
Filter Words

Words like saw, heard, watched, noticed, felt, could tell push the reader away from the moment.

"I saw a bright flash."

"A bright flash split the room."

"She heard footsteps."

"Footsteps hammered the hallway."

"He noticed the shadows."

"Shadows stretched and swallowed the corners."

"I felt scared."

"My stomach tightened."

Common filter list

saw • looked • watched • noticed • heard • listened • felt • realised • thought • wondered • could tell • seemed to

Adverb
Adverbs (-ly)

Adverbs often tell instead of show. Replace the -ly word with a stronger action.

"Liam spoke angrily."

"Liam slammed his fist on the table."

"She walked slowly."

"She dragged her shoes through the dust."

"He replied nervously."

"His voice cracked on the first word."

"They ran quickly."

"They bolted, feet smacking the pavement."

Adverbs to watch

quickly • slowly • suddenly • really • literally • basically • extremely • angrily • quietly • nervously

REPETITION
Repetition

Repeating the same strong word too close together sounds clunky. Swap it, combine, or re-shape the sentence.

Swap

"said" → "muttered / snapped" • "ran" → "bolted / sprinted" • "looked" → "glanced / peered"

Combine

Two short sentences → one longer sentence with a tighter flow.

Zoom in

Repeat less, describe more: add a sound, texture, temperature, or movement detail.

Example

"The wind howled. The wind pushed him. The wind froze his face."

"Wind howled through the gap, shoving him sideways and freezing his cheeks."
